Rules for Constructing Integer Constants

(a) An integer constant must have at least one digit.

(b) It must not have a decimal point.

(c) It can be any of any zero, positive or negative.

(d) If no sign precedes an integer constant, it is assumed to be positive.

(e) No commas or blanks are allowed within an integer constant.

(f) The allowable range for integer constant is -2147483648 to +2147483647.

EXAMPLE: 900, +782, -8000

Note : The range of integer constant depends up on compiler. For compilers like Visual Stdio, GCC it is -2147483648 to +2147483647, Whereas for compilers like Turbo C or Turbo C++ the range is -32768 to +327767.

Rules for Constructing Real Constants

Real constants are often called Floating Point constants. Real constants could be written in two form- Fraction form and Exponential form.

(a) An integer constant must have at least one digit.

(b) It must have a decimal point.

(c) It could be either positive or negative.

(d) Default sign is positive.

(e) No commas or blanks are allowed within an integer constant.

EXAMPLE: +327.90, 426.0, -32.0, -4.9567

Exponential form of real constant:

The exponential form is usually used if the value of the constant is either too small or too large. there is no restriction to use exponential form of real constant.

In Exponential form, the real constant is represented in two parts.

The part appearing before 'e' is called mantissa.

the part following 'e' is called exponent

Thus 0.000342 can be written in exponential form as 3.42e-4

which in normal arthmatic means 3.42 * 10^-4

Rules for constructing exponential form of real constants

(a) The mantissa part and the exponential part should be separated by a letter E or e.

(b) The mantissa part may have a positive or negative sign.

(c) Default sign of mantissa part is positive.

(d) The exponential must have at least one digit either positive or negative.

(e) Range of exponential form of real constants is -3.4e38 to 3.4e38.

EXAMPLE: +3.2e-5, 4.1e8, -0.2E+3, -3.2e-9

Rules for Constructing Character Constants

(a) A character constant is single alphabet, a single digit or a single special symbol.

(b) A special symbol must be enclosed within single inverted commas.

(c) Both inverted commas should point to left..

EXAMPLE: 'A', 'I', '7', '=', '9', '8', '%'
